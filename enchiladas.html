Enchiladas

Full enchiladas recipe with homemade sauce

Ingredients

Dry ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons of Flour
  • 1 tablespoon of Smoked Paprika (use Mild or Hot depending on how spicy you like it)
  • 2 teaspoons of Cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of Oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of Garlic Salt (or 1/2 of just salt!)

Rest of the ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of Olive Oil
  • 400 g of Chopped Tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon of cider vinegar
  • 0.5 Cabbage
  • 1 Red Pepper
  • 1 Vegetable stock cube
  • 50 g of Cheese
  • 4 Tortilla wraps

Steps

Enchilada Sauce

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of Olive Oil in a pan on a medium heat
  2. Once the oil is hot, pour in all the dry ingredients (3 tablespoons of Flour, 1 tablespoon of Smoked Paprika, 2 teaspoons of Cumin, 1 teaspoon of Oregano, 1 teaspoon of Garlic Salt) and start whisking/stirring (You can tell the oil is hot enough by putting a pinch of flour in and it should sizzle)
  3. After around 1 minute, pour in the 400 g of Chopped Tomatoes and stir until mixed. You can use a tiny bit of water to get any remaining tomato juice in the tin.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 3 minutes
  5. Stir in the 1 teaspoon of cider vinegar and then turn off the heat

Enchilada time

  1. Place a frying pan on a medium/low heat
  2. Cut up the 0.5 Cabbage and 1 Red Pepper into small chunks
  3. Put a large frying pan on a medium heat and put the small chunks of cauliflower and pepper in to soften for about 10 minutes
  4. Grate up the 50 g of Cheese and turn the oven on to 200c / 180c Fan / Gas Mark 6
  5. Once the cauliflower and red pepper is to your liking, pour in most of the sauce and cheese, leaving some back for the top of the enchiladas
  6. Split the mixture evenly into the 4 Tortilla wraps, roll them up and place together in a baking tray
  7. Pour the remaining sauce on top of the enchiladas and put some extra cheese on top
  8. Bake in the oven for 12 minutes
